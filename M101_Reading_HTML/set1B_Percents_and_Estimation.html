Percents

Percent means "parts per hundred." The below figure represents \(23\%\).

A large square has been partitioned into \(10\times 10 = 100\) equal parts. We have shaded \(23\) of the \(100\) possible little squares, or \(23\%\) of the total number of little squares.

To change a percent to a decimal, we need only remember that percent means "parts per hundred." Recalling Set 1A-Fractions, \(23\%=\dfrac{23}{100}\) or \(23\) parts out of \(100\). Dividing \(23\) by \(100\) yields \(23\%=\dfrac{23}{100}=0.23\). Recall that in the decimal representation, \(2\) is in the tenths position and \(3\) is in the hundredths position of the decimal \(0.23\).

Converting Between Percents and Decimals

Example 1B-1: Convert a Percent to a Decimal

Convert \(37.5\%\) into a decimal.
\(37.5\%\) Percent means parts per hundred: drop the percent symbol and write the \(37.5\) over \(100\)
\(\dfrac{37.5}{100}\) Dividing by 100 moves the decimal point \(2\) places left
\(.375\) Our Solution
Type .375 for \( .375 \) in WeBWorK

This last example motivates the following simple rule. To change a percent to a decimal, drop the percent symbol and move the decimal point two places to the left.

Example 1B-2: Convert a Percent to a Decimal

Convert \(2\frac{1}{2}\%\) into a decimal.
\(2\frac{1}{2}\%\) The fraction \(\frac{1}{2}=\frac{5}{10}=.5\)
\(2.5\%\) Drop the percent symbol and move the decimal point two places to the left
\(.025\) Our Solution

Changing a decimal to a percent is the exact opposite of changing a percent to a decimal: move the decimal point two places to the right and add a percent symbol.

Example 1B-3: Convert a Decimal to a Percent

Convert \(0.0725\) to a percent.
\(.0725\) Move the decimal point two places to the right and add a percent symbol
\(7.25\%\) Our Solution
Type 7.25% for \(7.25\% \) in WeBWorK

Example 1B-4: Convert a Decimal to a Percent

Convert \(1.025\) to a percent.
\(1.025\) Move the decimal point two places to the right and add a percent symbol
\(102.5\%\) Our Solution

Example 1B-5: Determine a Percent

Suppose that results of a survey revealed \(173\) out of \(325\) respondents had brown eyes. Give the percent of respondents having brown eyes.
\(172\) out of \(325\) Determine the fraction with brown eyes
\(\dfrac{172}{325}\) Compute the equivalent decimal: divide \(172\) by \(325\) on your calculator
\(0.52923...\) Move the decimal point two places to the right and add a percent symbol
\(52.923\%\) Our Solution: \(52.923\%\) of the respondents have brown eyes
It is generally a good idea to maintain \(3\) to \(5\) decimals of accuracy when submitting answers in WeBWorK

Determining a Percent of a Number

One type of percent problems involves determining a specific percent of a given number.

Example 1B-6: Determine a Percent of a Number

Find \(25\%\) of \(640\). Here of means multiplication
\(25\%\cdot 640\) Change \(25\%\) to a decimal
\(.25\cdot 640\) Multiply (check this with a calculator!)
\(160\) Our Solution

Example 1B-7: Determine a Percent of a Number

Find \(175\%\) of \(30\). Here of means multiplication
\(175\%\cdot 30\) Change \(175\%\) to a decimal
\(1.75\cdot 30\) Multiply
\(52.5\) Our Solution

Decimals on the Real Number Line

As a decimal, \(23\) parts out of \(100\) is \(23\%=\dfrac{23}{100}=0.23\). We see that \(100\%=\dfrac{100}{100}=1\) is all \(100\) parts out of \(100\). This means \(0.23\) is a number less than \(1\) but greater than \(0\) since \(0=\dfrac{0}{100}\) zero parts out of \(100\).

Example 1B-8: Decimals on the Number Line

Determine the location of the decimals on the number line.
\(2.5=2 + 0.5 = 2 + \dfrac{50}{100}= 2+\dfrac{1}{2}\)
is a number half-way between \(2\) and \(3\)
\(0.84=\dfrac{84}{100}\) is more than \(0.80=\dfrac{80}{100}\)
but less than \(0.90=\dfrac{90}{100}\) which means it
is a number between \(0.8\) and \(0.9\)

Rounding Decimals

This is a situation where our experience with whole numbers is valuable. Rounding a decimal number to a given place value or a given number of decimal places is very similar to rounding whole numbers.

Example 1B-9: Rounding to Tenths

Round \(6.7291\) to the nearest tenth. Find the tenths place: one place after the decimal
\(6.\color{red}{7}291\) \(\color{red}{7}\) is in the tenths place. The next digit is the trigger.
\(6.\color{red}{7}\color{blue}{2}91\) Since the trigger \(\color{blue}{2}\) is less than \(5\), we do not round up.
\(6.7\) Our Solution

Example 1B-10: Rounding to Hundredths

Round \(6.7291\) to the nearest hundredth. Find the hundredths place: two places after the decimal
\(6.7\color{red}{2}91\) \(\color{red}{2}\) is in the hundredths place. The next digit is the trigger.
\(6.7\color{red}{2}\color{blue}{9}1\) Since the trigger \(\color{blue}{9}\) is greater than \(5\), we round up.
\(6.73\) Our Solution
